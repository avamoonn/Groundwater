How to Use DFPS

To use DFPS, click the Green Run DFPS Button

Then use the form to enter the values appropriate for the system of interest

Use units specified in each field, and the tool will convert to consistent units for calculations.

Then, press the Submit button and results will appear below the input form.

Explanation of Outputs

The interactive outputs include several graphs and visualizations to illustrate the effects of groundwater pumping on streams and aquifers over time.

1. Stream Depletion Fraction (Qfraction) vs. Time

This graph shows the fraction of the pumping rate being drawn from the stream over time. Initially, groundwater provides most of the pumping demand, but as time progresses, the stream contributes a higher fraction. This reflects the increasing hydraulic connectivity between the well and the stream.

2. Stream Flow Rate (Discharge) vs. Time

This graph displays the decreasing discharge of the stream as more water is drawn into the well. The stream's flow stabilizes at a reduced rate over time, showing the hydraulic response to prolonged pumping.

3. Drawdown vs. Time (Observation Wells 1 and 2)

These graphs illustrate the lowering of the water table at two specific observation wells over time. The drawdown is influenced by proximity to the pumping well and the transmissivity of the aquifer.

4. W-E and S-N Cross-Sections of Drawdown

These cross-sectional graphs show the spatial variation in drawdown along west-east and south-north axes at selected times. They provide a detailed look at how drawdown changes across the aquifer in relation to the well and the stream.

5. Contour Map of Drawdown

The contour map represents the spatial distribution of drawdown across the entire aquifer at a specific time. It uses color coding to show the magnitude of drawdown, with closer proximity to the well showing higher drawdown values.

These visualizations, combined, provide a comprehensive understanding of groundwater dynamics, emphasizing the interaction between pumping wells, aquifers, and streams. If input errors occur, the tool provides helpful messages to ensure results are reliable and meaningful.
